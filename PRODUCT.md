# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two visitor archetypes share a single site. Both decide credibility fast, then either bounce or commit to the next click. The site's job is to make that next click obvious for each.

### The Buyer
A CTO, CDO, COO, or founder. Arrives from a Google search ("fractional AI lead Europe", "data strategy consultant", similar) or from a LinkedIn post. They have a problem. They want to know if Erlon can solve it, what it looks like to work with him, and how to start. Decision window: 30 to 60 seconds.

### The Recruiter
Internal TA or executive search. Arrives from Erlon's LinkedIn, a referral, or a Boolean search. They want to validate seniority, scope, and cultural fit before contacting him. Decision window: under 30 seconds.

### Shared context
Both audiences are senior. Both are scanning, not reading. Both will leave if the first surface looks generic. The page they land on must reward fast scanning with high-signal proof.

## Product Purpose

Turn cold attention into a qualified conversation within 90 seconds.

Success is measured by qualified outbound replies, recruiter contacts, and discovery-call requests, not by SEO rank or vanity metrics. SEO, analytics polish, and visual finesse are downstream concerns to be addressed later. The 90-second test is the only one that matters at launch.

The portfolio is the credibility engine. 32 case studies spanning 2013 to 2026, organized by career era (Contractor 2025-26, Director Data and AI at multinational gaming 2022-25, Senior Director at global consultancy 2022-23, Senior Manager at consultancy 2018-21, Director of Technology 2016), each in dual format: a Situation-Complication-Resolution brief for fast scanning, and a longer narrative for deeper signal. Everything else on the site exists to frame and route to this evidence.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **trusted, efficient, human-centric.**

- **Trusted** comes from quantified outcomes ("15% conversion uplift", "430% increase in monthly digital sales", "USD 50M annual recurring impact"), from rich client anonymization (sector + region + scale, not "company X"), and from voice-driven essays that show a working point of view.
- **Efficient** comes from page-level brevity, type-first composition, and from interfaces that respect the 30-second scan: no decorative chrome, no warmup paragraphs, no "let me tell you about myself" intros.
- **Human-centric** comes from the writing voice itself, especially in the longer-form case essays: lessons about people, not just systems. The portfolio file's preface ("Nothing that is worth doing can be done alone") is the keystone of this dimension.

Voice characteristics derived from Erlon's own portfolio prose:
- Confident without ceremony. Opinionated, never hedging.
- Editorial, observational, slightly dry. "Useful beats clever." "Hours are linear; products compound." "Detection without intervention is detection theatre."
- Math-rigor without arrogance (Bernhardsson template). Numbers carry weight; prose carries context.
- British understatement when it suits, plain declarative when it doesn't.

Voice handling for site copy that Erlon authored: em dashes in his drafts are visual separators, not stylistic signature. Normalize to `:`, `,`, or `.` based on grammatical fit when copy lands in UI.

## Anti-references

Direct call-outs from Erlon's competitive scan:

- **"Fractional CXO" agency sites** (chiefaiofficer.com, momentumhub.ai, and the broader category): SaaS-landing-page template, stock photos, logo soup, pricing-tier cards. These convert down-market. Erlon is building for the next bracket up; those visual cues will hurt the senior buyer and the recruiter alike.

General consultant-site patterns to refuse:

- **Wix or Squarespace template look:** full-bleed hero photo, three identical "What I do" columns, big rounded-corner testimonial quotes. This is the current site; the rebuild exits this lane entirely.
- **LinkedIn-clone portfolio:** skill bars, certifications grid, vertical career timeline. A CV pasted into a site is not a site. Explicitly per Erlon: "no skills bar graphics. They aged badly five years ago."
- **Conference-speaker aesthetic:** "BOOK ME" button, gradient hero, photos of the subject on stage, follower-count badges.
- **SaaS marketing tropes:** hero metric template (Big Number / Small Label), gradient text, glassmorphism, decorative card grids with icon-title-blurb.
- **The hero portrait:** large smiling executive photo above the fold. Even a good photo undercuts the editorial register.

Reflex-rejected design lanes (do not converge on the category default):

- "Personal site for tech exec → dark blue + sans-serif + center-aligned hero." Common, expected, indistinct. Avoid by going either lighter (editorial magazine) or more committed (typographic system with declared color strategy).

## Design Principles

1. **Dual audience, single voice.** The credibility scaffolding (positioning, proof, point of view) is shared across both Buyer and Recruiter. Only the conversion paths differ. One body of writing, two routes through it.

2. **90 seconds to qualified.** Every above-the-fold decision, every hero element, every nav choice is judged against this clock. If a section does not move a Buyer toward a discovery-call request or a Recruiter toward validation within the scan window, it is decoration and should be cut.

3. **Premium by subtraction.** Seniority signals through what is left out, not added on. Anti-targets are visual: SaaS-template chrome, logo soup, pricing tiers, stock photography. The senior bracket recognizes restraint. Compare Will Larson's "Work with me" page: tight, signals seniority by what it omits.

4. **Actively available, two productized tracks plus full-time.** Erlon is openly available across three engagement modes:
   - **Full-time executive role** (separate from productized): CDO, VP Data, Head of AI, or equivalent. Senior bracket, scaling or enterprise stage. Surfaced to Recruiters through the portfolio and bio; no productized landing page (FT search is a relationship process, not a buy-button).
   - **Fractional Executive** (productized, 3 tiers): senior strategic engagement. Erlon in the room.
   - **Embedded Builder** (productized, 3 tiers): senior hands-on engagement. Erlon in the codebase.
   
   The two productized tracks each have a gateway tier, a main offering, and a retainer (full scope in the Productized Offerings section). Dignity comes from specificity of criteria, not from simulated passivity. Erlon is choosing deliberately, not waiting hopefully.

5. **Math-rigor without arrogance.** Quantified outcomes carry more weight than narrative. Every case has at least one number; every claim is backed by the project that produced it. This is Bernhardsson's template, and it is exactly how Erlon already writes when he is being honest about his work.

## Productized Offerings

Two productized tracks, each with three tiers in a parallel structure: a low-commitment gateway, a main offering, and a retainer. Pricing is not displayed: every tier has a "request a quote" CTA. The Buyer's first click should propagate the chosen tier into a single shared contact form (hybrid model: per-tier CTAs converge to one form, with `tier of interest` pre-filled).

### Track A: Fractional Executive

The senior strategic engagement. Erlon in the decision room, structuring teams, setting strategy, advising, building organisations through the team.

#### Tier 1: Agentic AI Readiness Diagnostic (2 weeks)
**Who it's for:** A CEO, COO, or CTO who knows they need to act on AI but doesn't know what to ship first or how exposed they are under the EU AI Act.

**What they get:**
- Mapped inventory of AI use cases already in-flight (sanctioned + shadow)
- EU AI Act risk classification of each
- A 90-day action plan: what to kill, what to harden, what to pilot
- A 30-minute presentation to the leadership team

**Why it works:** Low commitment, fast turnaround. Output naturally creates demand for Tier 2. Diagnostic-to-engagement pattern of the best fractional CAIOs.

#### Tier 2: Fractional Head of AI (90-day sprint, renewable)
**Who it's for:** A scale-up or mid-market company that needs an executive in the room two days a week to set strategy, hire the right people, ship the first production agentic workflow, and stand up MLOps and governance from day one.

**What they get:**
- Embedded leadership presence (defined cadence)
- Strategy document + 90-day roadmap, owned end-to-end
- One production-grade agentic workflow shipped
- Governance scaffolding (data lineage, model cards, EU AI Act docs)
- A trained internal lead by day 90: **capability transfer is the deliverable**

**Why it works:** This is the bread and butter. Where applied math, leadership chops, and Iarandu's delivery muscle compound. The "I leave you with a trained successor" line is exceptional for buyers who fear consultant lock-in.

#### Tier 3: AI Operating Council (monthly retainer)
**Who it's for:** A board or exec team that already has AI capability but wants a senior independent voice in their quarterly cycle.

**What they get:**
- Monthly half-day strategy session
- Async review of major decisions
- Quarterly written outlook on regulation, vendor landscape, and team health

**Why it works:** Predictable monthly cash, low-time-cost. Signals "I am the kind of person companies retain" to anyone evaluating Erlon. Doubles as recruiter validation.

### Track B: Embedded Builder

The senior hands-on engagement. Erlon in the codebase, shipping production artifacts. Builder, shipper, executioner.

#### Tier 1: Technical Readiness Audit (2 weeks)
**Who it's for:** A CTO, Head of Engineering, or VP Data who suspects technical debt is blocking AI or data ambitions.

**What they get:**
- Stack assessment (data infrastructure + AI/ML layer + compliance posture)
- Bottleneck inventory: what hurts, what scales, what will break
- 60-day technical roadmap with the recommended sequence of builds
- 30-minute presentation to the technical leadership

**Why it works:** Low-commitment proof of competence. Output generates Tier 2 demand. Mirrors the Diagnostic of the Fractional track in the technical lens.

#### Tier 2: Embedded Builder Sprint (8 to 12 weeks, renewable)
**Who it's for:** A team that needs a senior practitioner hands-on to ship a specific production artifact: an agentic workflow, a data architecture refactor, an MLOps platform, a compliance framework (EU AI Act-ready), or a critical data engineering pipeline.

**What they get:**
- **Code shipped to production** (the artifact IS the deliverable)
- Architecture documentation
- One internal team member trained as owner
- Repo handed off with tests and CI/CD ready

**Why it works:** Pure shipping mode. Cadence of 3 days per week in the codebase. Capability transfer through the trained owner. The technical mirror of the Fractional Head of AI's "trained successor".

#### Tier 3: Architecture Retainer (monthly)
**Who it's for:** A technical team that wants ongoing senior architectural review without re-engagement overhead.

**What they get:**
- Bi-weekly architecture office hours (1 hour)
- Async review of major designs and PRs (48-hour turnaround)
- Quarterly stack health review (written + sync)

**Why it works:** Predictable cash, low time-cost, "senior architect on call" signal. Renewable monthly.

### Quote mechanic

- Each tier displays scope + duration + deliverables. No prices.
- Each tier has its own CTA button ("Start with the Diagnostic", "Discuss a Fractional engagement", "Join the Council", and analogous for Track B).
- All CTAs propagate the chosen tier into a single shared contact form with a `tier of interest` field pre-filled.
- The form captures: name, email, organisation, role, brief problem statement, preferred contact method. Light-touch; the conversation does the qualification, not the form.

### Binary choice presentation

The two productized tracks should appear as **parallel columns** on the "Work with me" page. The buyer's mental question — *"Do I need an executive in the room, or a builder in the codebase?"* — is answered by the layout itself before any reading happens. Both senior, both Erlon, different roles.

## Content Engine

A weekly+ cadence essay-driven funnel hosted on the canonical domain. Modeled on Simon Willison and Eugene Yan: own-site first, syndicate to LinkedIn, build search authority on `erlonrachi.com` over 12 to 18 months.

### Pillars (six)

1. **Agentic AI & Enterprise Architecture** — tiered maturity (Foundation / Workflows / Autonomy), MCP / ACP protocols, deployment patterns. The largest recurring theme in the existing inventory.
2. **AI Governance & EU AI Act** — Zero Trust by design, explainability, accountability, audit trails, regulatory commentary. Strong POV pillar.
3. **Data Infrastructure & Engineering Practice** — real-time inference, multi-cloud strategy, Data FinOps, observability, data quality frameworks. Demonstrates IC-level depth alongside executive lens.
4. **Leadership for CTOs and CDOs** — exec-level guidance for data and AI leaders. The "how to think about this" lane. Speaks directly to the Buyer archetype.
5. **The Human Factor** — teams, talent, transformation, capability transfer, mentorship. The educator and team-builder identity. Anchored by the philosophy "building people before building code".
6. **Field Notes** — conference recaps (London Tech Week, VivaTech), tool tests (Antigravity, frontier model evaluations), sector deep-dives (gaming, banking, CPG, regulated industries). Lighter cadence, network signal.

### Inventory & migration

- **Existing inventory:** 20 articles spanning June 2023 to November 2025, captured in `[db] articles Erlon Rachi.pdf` at project root. Cadence historically ~1 per month, with denser clusters in mid-2025.
- **Migration scope:** all 20 articles port to the new site. Older posts (2023, early 2024) carry visible publication dates so readers can place them in context. Long-tail SEO authority preserved from day one.
- **Per-article metadata required:** title, original publication date, pillar tag(s), one-line summary for the index, optional series marker (e.g., the "CTO & CDO Challenges" multi-part).

### Distribution

- **Canonical home:** `erlonrachi.com/writing/<slug>` (or `/essays`, decided in `/impeccable shape`).
- **Cross-post pattern:** publish first on own domain, syndicate to LinkedIn 24-48 hours later with a "originally published at..." link back. Concentrates SEO authority on the canonical URL.
- **No platforms-first writing:** LinkedIn becomes amplification, not the primary venue. Medium is not re-adopted.

### Index UX requirements (for `/impeccable shape` to honour)

- Categorised by pillar, browseable.
- Date stamps visible.
- Search across body text.
- RSS feed at standard location.
- Newsletter sign-up: deferred to v2; not blocking launch.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target:** WCAG 2.1 Level AA, fully met.
- **Motion reduction:** honor `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` site-wide. Disable scroll-driven animation, hover transitions over 100ms, and any decorative motion when the OS flag is set. Reduce to instant state changes.
- **Keyboard navigation:** full keyboard accessibility on the portfolio grid, filter controls (era / pillar tags), and project pages. Visible focus indicators on every interactive element. No focus traps.
- **Contrast:** 4.5:1 minimum for body text, 3:1 for large text, 3:1 for non-text UI components. Verify against final palette in DESIGN.md.
- **Heading hierarchy:** one H1 per page. Logical H2 / H3 structure on case pages and portfolio index (the auditor flagged the current site for shipping a single H1 with no sub-hierarchy; the rebuild fixes this from day one).
- **Reading order:** source order matches visual order. No CSS-only reordering that breaks screen-reader flow.
- **Image alternatives:** if a typographic-card design is selected (likely outcome of `/impeccable shape`), images may be minimal site-wide. Any decorative image gets `alt=""`; any meaningful image gets a real description.
- **Language:** `<html lang="en">` correct on every page. Site is English-only at launch.
